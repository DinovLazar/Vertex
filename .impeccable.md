# Vertex Consulting — Impeccable Design Context

Synthesized from `D-15_Website_Design_Document.md` and the phase-by-phase build notes in `src/_project-state/`. This is the design context for any `/impeccable:*` command run in this repo.

## Design Context

### Users
Macedonian small and mid-size business owners evaluating whether to hire a consultant or a marketing agency. Primary audience: 35–55-year-old owner-operators in Strumica and broader Macedonia who run established but stuck businesses. Secondary audience: select foreign clients operating in or entering the Macedonian market. They read the site on desktop during business hours and on mobile during evenings. They are skeptical of impressive-sounding agency copy and want proof of practical, hands-on results.

The job: decide quickly whether Vertex can help their specific problem, learn how the engagement works, and reach out with a low-stakes first conversation.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **direct, practical, confident**.

Two divisions with intentionally different energy inside a shared skeleton:
- **Vertex Consulting** — serious, premium, no-nonsense authority. Goran-led. Monochrome palette. Writing like "we roll up our sleeves and work alongside you until the results are real."
- **Vertex Marketing** — creative, energetic, bold expression. Lazar/Petar/Andrej. Lavender palette. Writing like "young, bold, and relentless."

The unifying brand voice across both: no jargon, no fluff, no corporate register. Prose is conversational but punchy ("Memory breaks, systems do not"). The site should feel like a confident small shop, not a faceless agency.

### Aesthetic Direction
Theme is **dark mode primary** — the site is consumed during focused business evaluation, often late in the day. Headings in a sharp, geometric sans (currently Sora); body in a refined humanist sans (currently DM Sans).

Division theming via CSS variables: `[data-division="consulting"]` → monochrome grays; `[data-division="marketing"]` → lavender; default (homepage, About, Contact, Blog, Privacy) → a shared brand-blue accent.

Anti-references: bad agency websites that lean on gradient text, cyan-on-dark glowing accents, stock hero photos, generic service-icon grids, and templated "trusted by" logo strips.

Animated backgrounds on hero-dense pages (Silk on home, Plasma on marketing, GridMotion on consulting) give the site a signature visual memory hook — these are the one thing a returning visitor should remember.

### Design Principles
1. **Typography drives the hierarchy.** Large type contrast ratios, confident headlines, tight bodies. No decorative gradient fills on text.
2. **Division theming is structural, not decorative.** Consulting vs Marketing vs Shared each have distinct accent colors + surface tints, applied via `var(--division-*)` tokens. Components never hardcode per-division colors.
3. **Motion is for state changes, not decoration.** Hero entrances, stagger reveals on scroll, and the language toggle respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Hover lifts are subtle (`y: -4`, not `scale: 1.1`).
4. **Bilingual from day one.** Every user-facing string lives in `messages/{en,mk}.json`. MK is ~10,000 words of translated prose as of Phase 15F; the Cyrillic font gap is currently flagged as a launch blocker.
5. **Fewer components, done with care.** The `src/components/sections/` library is 18 components reused heavily. Service pages, consulting/marketing landings, and the About/Contact pages all compose from the same set — keep this discipline.

### Accessibility & Inclusion
- Target WCAG AA across the site.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected in both the CSS reduced-motion block and Motion's `MotionConfig reducedMotion="user"`.
- Forms use real `<label>` + `id` pairings, with MK validation errors wired through `useTranslations`.
- No known audience-specific accommodations beyond bilingual + reduced-motion support.
